Profile

Research experience in computational modeling, numerical simulation, and scientific computing, involving both physics-based and data-driven approaches. Work includes the development and application of numerical methods, Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), and computational frameworks for solving complex problems in classical and theoretical physics. Research interests encompass the mathematical foundations of artificial intelligence, symmetry transformations, algebraic structures, and geometric group theory, together with their applications to physical modeling. Current investigations include quantum spacetime, discrete spacetime models, gravitational quantization, and computational approaches to fundamental problems in quantum gravity and mathematical physics.
